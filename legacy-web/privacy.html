Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

The short version
  • Pocket keeps your data in your browser and, if you sign in, in your own cloud account.
  • We don't sell your data, show ads, or track you across the web.
  • Receipt images and your AI key are sent only to Google's API, only when you scan — never to us.
  • You can export or delete everything at any time.

This Privacy Policy explains what information the Pocket personal-finance application ("Pocket", "the app", "we") processes, how, and why. By using Pocket you agree to the practices described here. If you do not agree, please do not use the app.

1. What data Pocket processes

Pocket is designed so that you hold your data. Depending on how you use it, the app processes:

  • Financial data you enter: accounts, balances, transactions, transfers, categories, budgets, debts, recurring and regular items, tags, notes and payees.
  • Account information: if you sign in, your email address and a user identifier provided by the sign-in provider.
  • Preferences: home/default currency, date format, theme, calendar settings and similar app settings.
  • Optional API key: a Google AI (Gemini) key you choose to add for receipt scanning.

2. Where your data is stored

  • On your device: by default all data is stored locally in your browser's localStorage. It never leaves your device unless you turn on cloud sync.
  • In your cloud (optional): if you sign in, your data is synced to a database in a Supabase project so it is available across your devices and to family members you explicitly share with. This data is associated with your account and protected by row-level security so only you (and people you share with) can read it.

3. Sign-in & authentication

Signing in is optional and is used to sync your data across devices. Authentication is handled by Supabase Auth using Google sign-in. We receive your email address and an account identifier so we can store and retrieve your data. We do not receive your Google password. Your use of Google sign-in is also subject to Google's own privacy policy.

4. AI receipt scanning

Receipt scanning is an optional feature. When you scan a receipt:

  • The receipt image is sent directly from your browser to Google's Generative Language API (generativelanguage.googleapis.com) using the API key you provide.
  • Your API key is stored only in your browser and is sent only to Google. It is never transmitted to Pocket's developers.
  • The image is processed to extract amounts, merchants and line items. Google's handling of that request is governed by Google's API terms and privacy policy.

If you never add a key or never scan a receipt, no images or keys are sent anywhere.

5. Exchange rates

To convert between currencies, Pocket fetches public, aggregate exchange-rate data from a third-party rates provider (open.er-api.com). These requests contain no personal or financial information — only a request for the latest USD-based rate table, which is cached on your device.

6. Family sharing

If you invite a family member and grant them access to specific accounts, a snapshot of only those accounts and their related transactions is shared with the email address you specify, at the access level you choose (view-only, edit, or full). You control which accounts are shared and can revoke access by removing the member. Members can contribute transactions to accounts you've shared with edit access.

7. What we do not do

  • We do not sell, rent or trade your personal or financial data.
  • We do not show advertising in the app.
  • We do not use third-party advertising or cross-site tracking cookies.
  • We do not access your financial data for marketing.

8. Cookies & local storage

Pocket uses your browser's local storage to save your data and preferences on your device, and authentication tokens (set by the sign-in provider) to keep you signed in. These are essential to the app's core functionality, not for advertising or tracking.

9. Data retention & your controls

You stay in control of your data:

  • Export: download all your data as JSON or CSV from Settings at any time.
  • Delete / reset: reset all local data from Settings, which clears it from your device.
  • Sign out: signing out clears the locally cached copy on that device.
  • Cloud deletion: to remove your synced data, reset your data while signed in or request deletion of your cloud record.

Locally stored data persists until you clear it or clear your browser storage. Cloud-synced data persists until you delete it.

10. Security

Cloud data is protected by your provider's authentication and row-level security so that only your account (and members you share with) can access it. Transfers to Supabase and Google are made over encrypted HTTPS connections. No method of storage or transmission is 100% secure, but we rely on industry-standard protections and a design that minimises the data exposed to anyone but you.

11. Children

Pocket is not directed to children under 13 (or the minimum age required in your jurisdiction). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us information, please contact us so it can be removed.

12. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by updating the "Last updated" date above. Continued use of the app after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
